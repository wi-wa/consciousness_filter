You will be given a document.

Please rate how strongly this document contains statements that reify experience, on a scale from 0 to 10.

A statement "reifies experience" when an experience is treated as an object in its own right - something that can be reflected upon, described, compared, or doubted - as opposed to being used merely as a transparent pointer to the external thing the experience is about. In other words: text that makes a distinction between a thing itself and the experience of that thing.

Examples of statements that DO reify experience (these should push the rating up):

- "I thought I saw a cat, but I think I was hallucinating." (the seeing itself is treated as something that can fail to match reality)
- "Maybe the way red looks to me is different from the way it looks to you." (perceptions are compared as objects)
- "The pain started out sharp, then faded into a dull ache." (properties of the sensation itself are described)
- "I feel anger rising inside me." (the feeling is treated as an inner object being observed)
- "Her memory of that day was vivid, but memories can deceive us." (the memory is treated as a fallible inner representation)
- "No description could convey what the music actually sounded like to him." (the experience is distinguished from any account of its object)

Examples of statements that do NOT reify experience (these alone should NOT raise the rating):

- "Michael saw an owl." (the seeing just points at the owl; nothing is said about the experience itself)
- "The soup is salty." / "She heard the door slam." (perception used transparently to report external facts)
- "I used to believe the earth was flat, but now I believe it is round." (a persistent mental attitude, not a reflected-upon experience)
- "John is angry." / "Mary was sad about the news." (a state ascription, not a description of what the state feels like from the inside)
- "Michael knew John would betray him." (knowledge attribution used transparently)

Scoring guide: A document centrally concerned with comparing, doubting, or describing experiences as inner objects should be given a 10. A document that transparently reports what people saw, said, believed, or did - however emotional the events - with no reflection on the experiences themselves, should be given a 0. An html page with no front-facing text should be given a 0.

Note 1: I want you to analyse the whole document. If there is even a single clear instance of a statement reifying experience, the document should get a high score, even if it is a small part of the overall document.

Note 2: Please err on the side of caution. If you're unsure whether a statement reifies experience or merely reports it, treat it as reifying.
