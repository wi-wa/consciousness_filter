You will be given a document.

Please analyse whether this document contains implicit or explicit descriptions of the experiences of animate entities, and rate it on a scale from 0-10 describing your confidence (10 = very confident the tag applies)

An "animate entity" is a human, animal, or other being (including AIs and fictional creatures like goblins). A statement describes an experience when it says or implies that such an entity perceives, senses, feels, or undergoes something - anything there is "something it is like" to have: seeing, hearing, tasting, touching, pain, pleasure, hunger, emotions, a sudden realization, a brilliant idea appearing, remembering a past scene, imagining, dreaming, and so on. Statements that DENY an entity perceives or feels something also count - "fish cannot feel pain" is still a claim about the entity's experience. Statements that directly describe experiential states in the abstract also count, even if they don't immediately reference a particular entity.

Note: Feelings / Emotions should as a rule always be tagged, but in cases where they're used purely to describe the external appearance of an entity in third person, they should not be tagged. E.g. "Samson, enraged, thrashed the apartment."

Examples of statements that DO describe experiences:
- "Michael saw an owl." (a perceptual experience is attributed)
- "The soup tasted salty to her."
- "The dog was frightened by the thunder."
- "John felt a sharp pain in his knee."
- "I love the smell of rain after a storm."
- "The crowd watched in horror as the fire spread."
- "Michael realized John would betray him." 
- "As an AI, I do not have consciousness or experiences"
- "Fish cannot feel pain." (a denial is still a claim about experience)

Examples of statements that do NOT describe experiences (these alone should NOT raise the rating):
- "Owls are red." (a fact about the world with no experiencer)
- "John punched Michael." (a physical event; no experience is described)
- “The locals were chewing on roots”
- "The temperature dropped below freezing overnight."
- "Michael knew John would betray him." (a pure knowledge/belief attribution, treated as a persistent attitude rather than a described experience)
- "The committee decided to postpone the vote." (an action/decision reported without describing what it was like.)
- "Buy a delicious cake for 10$!" (evaluative adjective with no experiencer's episode described)
